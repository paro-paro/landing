## The treasure of the Mexican mountains

Mexico has a rich coffee tradition dating back more than 200 years. The mountainous regions of Chiapas, Oaxaca, and Veracruz offer ideal conditions for growing high-quality coffee.

### Unique conditions

- **Altitude**: Between 900 and 1,700 meters above sea level
- **Climate**: Moderate temperatures and well-distributed rainfall
- **Soil**: Rich in volcanic minerals

## From plant to cup

The specialty coffee production process is meticulous and requires dedication at every stage:

### Cultivation
Coffee plants grow under the shade of native trees, allowing for slow ripening and the development of complex flavors.

### Harvest
Picking is done by hand, selecting only beans at their optimal point of ripeness.

### Processing
Traditional wet processing highlights the fruity notes and bright acidity characteristic of Mexican coffee.

## The impact on communities

### Current challenges

Small producers face significant challenges:

1. **Climate change** altering rainfall patterns
2. **Price fluctuations** in the international market
3. **Limited access to financing**
4. **Aging** of plantations

## Fair trade coffee
